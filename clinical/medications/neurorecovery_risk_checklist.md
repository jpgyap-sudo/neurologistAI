# Neurorecovery Medication Risk Checklist

Use this as a structured physician-discussion checklist, not as medication advice.

## Medication classes that may suppress arousal or interfere with rehab

- Benzodiazepines
- Sedative hypnotics
- High-dose antipsychotics / dopamine antagonists
- Centrally acting anticholinergics
- Sedating antiepileptics
- High-dose baclofen or tizanidine
- Excess antihypertensive burden causing hypotension or low cerebral perfusion

## Neurostimulant discussion topics

- Amantadine dose, timing, renal adjustment, seizure risk, agitation risk.
- Methylphenidate/modafinil only under specialist supervision.
- Objective arousal endpoints before/after medication changes.

## Physician questions

1. Could any current medication be suppressing arousal or therapy participation?
2. Are BP targets appropriate given large deep hemorrhage recovery and perfusion needs?
3. Is amantadine dose/timing optimized for wakefulness while avoiding adverse effects?
4. Are sedating antispasticity or antiepileptic agents necessary at current dose?
