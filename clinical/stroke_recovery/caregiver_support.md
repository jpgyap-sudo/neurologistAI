# Caregiver Support in Stroke Recovery

## Caregiver Burden Assessment

- **Zarit Burden Interview (ZBI):** 22-item questionnaire assessing psychological, physical, social, and financial burden.
  - Score 0–20: little or no burden.
  - Score 21–40: mild to moderate burden.
  - Score 41–60: moderate to severe burden.
  - Score 61–88: severe burden.
- Administer at discharge, 1 month, and 3 months post-stroke; earlier if caregiver distress is evident.
- High ZBI scores predict patient hospital readmission, institutionalization, and worsening patient outcomes.

## Mental Health Screening for Caregivers

| Tool | Focus | Action threshold |
|------|-------|-----------------|
| Patient Health Questionnaire-2 (PHQ-2) | Depression | Score ≥3 warrants PHQ-9 or referral |
| Generalized Anxiety Disorder-2 (GAD-2) | Anxiety | Score ≥3 warrants GAD-7 or referral |
| Brief COPE | Coping strategies | Identify maladaptive coping (denial, substance use, behavioral disengagement) |

- Screen caregivers concurrently with patient follow-up appointments.
- Refer to social work, psychology, or psychiatry when screening is positive or when caregiver reports feeling overwhelmed.

## Respite Care and Community Resources

- **In-home respite:** trained aides or volunteers provide temporary relief while caregiver steps away.
- **Adult day programs:** structured activities and supervision for patients; allows caregivers to work or rest.
- **Short-term institutional respite:** nursing facility or rehabilitation center placement for 1–2 weeks.
- **Community resources:** stroke support groups (AHA/ASA, local stroke associations), caregiver helplines, online forums, and financial aid programs.
- Document resource referrals in the medical record and confirm caregiver received contact information.

## Training for Caregivers

| Domain | Core skills | Verification |
|--------|------------|--------------|
| Safe transfers | Bed-to-chair, chair-to-toilet; use of gait belt, slide board, or mechanical lift | Return demonstration before discharge |
| Feeding | Safe swallowing precautions (texture modification, positioning, pacing); recognition of aspiration signs | Teach-back method; provide written instructions |
| Medication administration | Timing, dosing, adherence aids (pillboxes, alarms); recognition of side effects (bleeding, hypotension, sedation) | Medication reconciliation with caregiver present; provide updated list |
| Skin care | Repositioning schedule, pressure relief, moisture barrier use | Checklist and schedule provided |
| Emergency recognition | Stroke warning signs (FAST), when to call emergency services | Written action plan and emergency contacts |

- Schedule a dedicated caregiver education session prior to discharge.
- Provide multilingual materials when indicated.

## Safety

- Caregiver burnout is an independent risk factor for patient readmission, medication errors, and delayed medical attention for complications.
- If a caregiver is unable to meet care demands, escalate to case management early to explore home health services, assisted living, or long-term care options.
- Maintain cultural sensitivity: family decision-making dynamics vary; involve designated family spokespersons and interpreters as needed.
