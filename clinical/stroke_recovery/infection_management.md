# Infection Management in Stroke / DoC

⚠️ This document is clinical decision support. It does not replace physician judgment or institutional protocols.

## Common Infections

| Infection | Risk Factors / Clues |
|-----------|----------------------|
| UTI | Indwelling catheter, incontinence, incomplete voiding |
| Pneumonia | Aspiration, intubation, immobility, poor oral hygiene |
| Shunt infection | VP/VA shunt present; fever + meningeal signs (if discernible) |
| Bloodstream infection | Central lines, pressure injuries, surgical sites |

## Diagnostic Clues in Non-Verbal Patients

- **Fever pattern**: spiking vs. low-grade; consider drug fever and central fever in neuro patients.
- **Autonomic changes**: unexplained tachycardia, hypertension, diaphoresis, or new rigidity.
- **Lab trends**: rising WBC, left shift, procalcitonin elevation, new acidosis.
- **Imaging**: new or worsening infiltrate on chest X-ray/CT; shunt series abnormalities.
- **Exam changes**: worsening arousal, new nystagmus, pupillary changes (shunt infection).

## Antibiotic Stewardship

- Avoid indwelling urinary catheters; use intermittent catheterization when feasible.
- Remove lines and drains as soon as no longer indicated.
- Minimize sedating medications that mask infection signs or depress respiration.
- Do not treat asymptomatic bacteriuria (except pregnancy or prior to urologic procedure).
- Narrow antibiotics once cultures and sensitivities return.

## Shunt Infection Workup

- **Clinical**: Fever, neck stiffness, altered mental status, shunt tract erythema/swelling, abdominal pain (VP shunt).
- **CSF analysis**: Cell count with differential, glucose, protein, Gram stain, culture.
- **Shunt tap**: Performed by neurosurgery; send CSF for studies above plus PCR if indicated.
- **Imaging**: Shunt series X-rays to assess hardware integrity; head CT for ventricular size changes.
- **Management**: Usually requires shunt externalization/removal + IV antibiotics; coordinate with neurosurgery.

## Ventilator-Associated Pneumonia (VAP) Bundle

- Elevate head of bed 30–45°.
- Daily sedation interruption and spontaneous awakening trials.
- Oral care with chlorhexidine (per protocol).
- DVT prophylaxis.
- Peptic ulcer prophylaxis when indicated.
- Daily assessment of extubation readiness.
