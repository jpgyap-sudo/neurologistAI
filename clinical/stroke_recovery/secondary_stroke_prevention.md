# Secondary Stroke Prevention

⚠️ This document is clinical decision support. It does not replace physician judgment or institutional protocols.

## Antiplatelet vs Anticoagulation Based on Etiology

| Etiology | Preferred Strategy | Notes |
|----------|-------------------|-------|
| Atrial fibrillation (cardioembolic) | Anticoagulation (DOAC or warfarin) | Start when safe after hemorrhagic conversion or ICH ruled out |
| Large artery atherosclerosis / carotid stenosis | Antiplatelet (aspirin ± clopidogrel short term) | Consider revascularization if symptomatic severe stenosis |
| Small vessel disease (lacunar) | Antiplatelet (aspirin or clopidogrel) | Aggressive risk factor control |
| Cryptogenic / embolic stroke of undetermined source | Antiplatelet; consider loop recorder for occult AF | Reassess etiology periodically |

- Dual antiplatelet therapy (DAPT): generally limited to 21–90 days post-minor stroke/TIA per CHANCE/POINT data, then transition to single agent.

## Blood Pressure Targets

- **Ischemic stroke**: SBP <130 mmHg and DBP <80 mmHg (AHA/ASA target).
- **Hemorrhagic stroke**: individualized; avoid SBP <120 mmHg if it compromises cerebral perfusion. Typical post-ICH goal SBP 130–150 mmHg initially, then tighten as tolerated.
- Reassess orthostatic tolerance and cerebral perfusion in severe brain injury.

## Lipid Management

- **High-intensity statin** for all atherosclerotic ischemic stroke (atorvastatin 40–80 mg or rosuvastatin 20–40 mg).
- Goal LDL <70 mg/dL (1.8 mmol/L) in very high-risk patients; <100 mg/dL minimum.
- Add ezetimibe or PCSK9 inhibitor if goals not met on maximally tolerated statin.
- Check LFTs and CK if muscle symptoms arise.

## Diabetes

- Target HbA1c <7% for most patients; individualize if hypoglycemia risk or limited life expectancy.
- Avoid hypoglycemia—neuroglycopenia worsens brain injury.
- Prefer agents with low hypoglycemia risk if indicated (SGLT2i, GLP-1 RA, DPP-4i).

## Lifestyle Interventions

| Domain | Recommendation |
|--------|----------------|
| Smoking | Absolute cessation; offer pharmacotherapy + counseling |
| Alcohol | Limit or abstain; avoid binge drinking |
| Exercise | Graduated physical activity as rehab tolerates; aim for ≥150 min/week moderate-intensity eventually |
| Diet | Mediterranean-style diet (olive oil, nuts, fish, vegetables); reduce sodium (<2.3 g/day) |
