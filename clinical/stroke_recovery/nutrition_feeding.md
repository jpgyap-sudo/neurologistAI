# Nutrition and Feeding in Stroke Recovery

⚠️ This document is clinical decision support. It does not replace physician judgment or institutional protocols.

## Swallow Screening

- Perform within 24 hours of admission or as soon as clinically stable.
- **Gugging Swallowing Screen (GUSS)**:
  - Stepwise progression: indirect swallow → direct swallow with semisolid → liquid → solid.
  - Stop at any step if aspiration signs present (coughing, voice change, SpO₂ drop ≥2%).
- **Volume–Viscosity Swallow Test (V-VST)**:
  - Tests multiple volumes and viscosities.
  - Identifies impaired safety (penetration/aspiration) and efficacy (residue).
- Keep patient NPO until screen is passed or alternative access is secured.

## Indications for NG vs PEG Tube Feeding

| Factor | NG Tube | PEG Tube |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Expected duration | <2–4 weeks | >2–4 weeks or prolonged DoC |
| Aspiration risk with oral intake | Temporary bridge | Long-term enteral access |
| Gastric motility | Usually functional | May add jejunal extension if delayed gastric emptying |
| Surgical/anesthetic risk | Lower | Requires endoscopy/IR; assess sedation risk |

- Transition from NG to PEG if enteral nutrition anticipated beyond 2–4 weeks or if repeated tube dislodgement occurs.
- Do not delay nutrition beyond 24–48 hours in severe stroke if oral intake is unsafe.

## Aspiration Risk Stratification

- **High risk**: Reduced consciousness, absent gag/cough, delayed swallow initiation, severe dysarthria, recurrent pneumonia.
- **Mitigation**:
  - Elevate head of bed to 30–45° during feeds and for 30–60 minutes after.
  - Use post-pyloric or jejunal feeding if recurrent aspiration on gastric feeds.
  - Regular oral care to reduce bacterial load.

## Refeeding Syndrome Prevention

- Risk highest in malnourished, alcoholic, or chronically ill patients.
- **Prevention bundle**:
  - Check and replete phosphate, magnesium, potassium before starting feeds.
  - Start at low rate (e.g., 10–20 mL/hr) and advance gradually over 3–4 days.
  - Monitor electrolytes q12–24h for first 72 hours.
  - Supplement thiamine before glucose-containing feeds if at risk.

## Enteral Nutrition Formulas for Neuro Patients

- **High-protein formulas** (≥20% calories from protein): support wound healing and attenuate muscle loss.
- **Immune-modulating formulas** (arginine, omega-3 fatty acids, nucleotides): may be considered in critically ill; evidence mixed—use per institutional protocol.
- **Fiber-containing formulas** to reduce diarrhea/constipation.
- **Caloric target**: 25–30 kcal/kg/day (adjusted for obesity/underweight); protein 1.2–2.0 g/kg/day.

## Timeline: When to Reassess Oral Intake

- Re-evaluate swallow weekly or when consciousness improves.
- Repeat instrumental assessment (VFSS or FEES) if clinical status changes.
- Do not keep patient NPO indefinitely without proactive reassessment.
