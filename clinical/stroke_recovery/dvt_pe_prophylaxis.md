# DVT/PE Prophylaxis in Stroke

⚠️ This document is clinical decision support. It does not replace physician judgment or institutional protocols.

## Risk Stratification

- All immobilized stroke patients are at increased VTE risk.
- **Caprini score basics** (relevant components in acute stroke):
  - Age 41–60 (+1), 61–74 (+2), ≥75 (+3)
  - Immobility >72 hours (+2)
  - Central venous access (+2)
  - Active cancer (+2)
  - Prior VTE (+3)
  - Thrombophilia (+3)
- Higher scores favor pharmacologic prophylaxis if no bleeding contraindication.

## Pharmacologic Prophylaxis

| Agent | Dosing (prophylaxis) | Renal Considerations | Notes |
|-------|----------------------|----------------------|-------|
| Enoxaparin (LMWH) | 40 mg SC daily (or 30 mg SC q12h in high-risk/bariatric) | Reduce to 30 mg daily if CrCl <30 mL/min; avoid if severe renal failure | Preferred in many stroke protocols |
| Unfractionated heparin (UFH) | 5000 units SC q8–12h | No renal adjustment | Preferred if renal impairment, urgent procedures anticipated, or CRRT |
| DOACs (e.g., apixaban, rivaroxaban) | See institutional protocol for prophylaxis dosing | Renal dose adjustment required; avoid if CrCl <15–30 mL/min depending on agent | Generally used for treatment rather than primary prophylaxis in acute stroke; evidence evolving |

- Initiate as early as safe after stroke onset (often 24–48 hours if no hemorrhagic expansion).

## Mechanical Prophylaxis

- **Intermittent pneumatic compression (IPC) / sequential compression devices (SCDs)**
  - Apply to bilateral lower extremities.
  - Use when pharmacologic prophylaxis is contraindicated or as adjunct.
  - Ensure proper fit; check skin integrity daily.
- **Early mobilization** when hemodynamically and neurologically appropriate.

## Contraindications

- Active bleeding or uncontrolled coagulopathy
- Recent hemorrhagic stroke: hold pharmacologic prophylaxis until hematoma stability confirmed (often 24–72 hours, individualized by neurosurgery/neurology)
- Severe thrombocytopenia (e.g., platelets <50 ×10⁹/L per institutional threshold)
- Recent intracranial surgery with ongoing CSF leak or high bleeding risk
- Known heparin-induced thrombocytopenia (HIT): avoid heparins; use non-heparin alternatives if indicated

## Transition to Therapeutic Anticoagulation if DVT/PE Diagnosed

- Confirm with duplex ultrasound (DVT) or CT pulmonary angiography (PE).
- If PE or proximal DVT:
  - Transition from prophylaxis to **therapeutic anticoagulation** if no active bleeding.
  - Agent selection depends on renal function, timing from hemorrhagic stroke, and patient stability.
  - In recent hemorrhagic stroke, weigh risk of hematoma expansion vs. clot burden; consider IVC filter if anticoagulation unsafe.
- Re-image hemorrhagic lesions if therapeutic anticoagulation started within 1–2 weeks of bleed.
