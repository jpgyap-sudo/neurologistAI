# Imaging Review Checklist: NPH vs Hydrocephalus Ex Vacuo

## CT/MRI report wording
Check if report says:
- Ventriculomegaly
- Brain atrophy
- Widened sulci
- Tight sulci / sulcal effacement
- DESH
- Encephalomalacia
- Gliosis
- Old hemorrhage cavity
- Infarction / softening lesion
- Periventricular edema
- Hydrocephalus
- Obstructive hydrocephalus

## Axial review
- Are lateral ventricles symmetric?
- Is one lateral ventricle enlarged more than the other?
- Does enlargement match prior lesion territory?
- Are cortical sulci globally widened?
- Are high-convexity sulci visible and widened?
- Are Sylvian fissures enlarged?

## Coronal review
- Is the callosal angle narrow? Typical NPH often has smaller callosal angle.
- Are high-convexity sulci tight?
- Is there DESH?

## Sagittal review
- Is aqueduct obstruction suspected?
- Is the fourth ventricle enlarged or normal?
- Is there crowding at the vertex?

## Interpretation rule
- Tight vertex + enlarged Sylvian fissures + symmetric ventriculomegaly → NPH/DESH pattern.
- Widened sulci + asymmetric ventricles + lesion-matched expansion → ex vacuo pattern.
