# Hydrocephalus Ex Vacuo Rule Scaffold

Hydrocephalus ex vacuo is compensatory enlargement of CSF spaces due to loss of brain parenchymal volume. It is not primarily a CSF circulation/absorption disorder.

## Features favoring ex vacuo

- Ventricular enlargement adjacent to encephalomalacia, gliosis, infarction, hemorrhagic cavity, or post-surgical tissue loss.
- Asymmetric ventricular enlargement matching side of structural injury.
- Widened cortical sulci and fissures consistent with generalized or regional atrophy.
- No convincing high-convexity sulcal tightness.
- No clear DESH pattern.
- No meaningful clinical improvement after high-volume lumbar puncture, if performed correctly and assessed objectively.
- Stable ventriculomegaly across serial scans.

## Features against pure ex vacuo / consider mixed process

- Progressive ventricular enlargement out of proportion to atrophy.
- Tight high-convexity sulci with enlarged Sylvian fissures.
- Narrow callosal angle on standardized coronal plane.
- Transependymal CSF edema/periventricular FLAIR hyperintensity.
- Objective gait/cognition/urinary response after CSF drainage.

## Output rule
Never call ex vacuo with high confidence using one sign alone. Use convergent evidence:
- lesion-side anatomy
- sulcal pattern
- serial stability
- drainage response
- clinical syndrome correlation
