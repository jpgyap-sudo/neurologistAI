# NPH / Communicating Hydrocephalus Rule Scaffold

This file provides structured features that may support NPH or communicating hydrocephalus morphology. It is not diagnostic by itself.

## Imaging features supporting NPH-like morphology

- Ventriculomegaly disproportionate to cortical atrophy.
- Tight high-convexity and medial parietal sulci.
- Enlarged Sylvian fissures with DESH pattern.
- Reduced callosal angle on correct coronal plane.
- Temporal horn enlargement not explained by hippocampal atrophy.
- Periventricular FLAIR signal compatible with transependymal CSF flow, if present.

## Clinical features supporting NPH syndrome

- Gait impairment, typically magnetic/apraxic gait.
- Cognitive impairment.
- Urinary urgency/incontinence.
- Objective improvement after CSF tap test or lumbar drainage.

## Cautions in severe stroke/MCS

- Classic NPH clinical triad may be impossible to assess in MCS.
- Ventricular enlargement after large hemorrhage/infarction is often ex-vacuo.
- Shunt responsiveness is less predictable when symptoms are dominated by destructive brain injury.
