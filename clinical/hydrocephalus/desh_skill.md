# Skill: DESH Assessment

## Overview
DESH (disproportionately enlarged subarachnoid-space hydrocephalus) is an imaging pattern used to support normal pressure hydrocephalus (NPH) and differentiate it from ex vacuo ventriculomegaly.

---

## Input
- Axial and/or coronal CT or MRI. Optional: Evans index measurement for correlation.

---

## Process
1. Review axial images for ventriculomegaly out of proportion to sulcal enlargement.
2. Assess Sylvian fissures: are they disproportionately enlarged?
3. Assess high-convexity sulci: are they tight or effaced?
4. Confirm relatively symmetric communicating hydrocephalus pattern.
5. Correlate with narrow callosal angle if available.

---

## Output
- **DESH** present / absent
- **Confidence Level**: high / moderate / low

---

## Validation Checklist
- [ ] Enlarged Sylvian fissures identified
- [ ] Tight high-convexity sulci identified
- [ ] Ventriculomegaly out of proportion to atrophy
- [ ] Symmetry assessed
- [ ] Narrow callosal angle noted (if available)

---

## Clinical Interpretation
| DESH | Interpretation |
|----------------|----------------|
| **Present** | Supports **NPH morphology** |
| **Absent** | Does not exclude NPH; consider other features |
| **Symmetric + tight convexity + wide Sylvian** | Classic DESH |

### Important Caveats
- In **asymmetric post-hemorrhagic ventricular distortion**, DESH interpretation is less reliable. Always correlate with:
  - **Evans index**
  - **Callosal angle**
  - **Clinical triad** (gait apraxia, incontinence, dementia)
  - **CSF tap test / ELD response**
