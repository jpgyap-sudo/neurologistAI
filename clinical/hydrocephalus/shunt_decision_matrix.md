# VP Shunt Decision Matrix

## Evidence supporting shunt-responsive NPH
- Progressive ventricular enlargement out of proportion to atrophy
- Tight high-convexity sulci
- Disproportionately enlarged Sylvian fissures / DESH
- Callosal angle < 90° on correct coronal AC-PC plane
- Transependymal CSF flow/edema on FLAIR
- Objective improvement after large-volume LP or lumbar drainage

## Evidence supporting hydrocephalus ex-vacuo
- Ventricular enlargement adjacent to encephalomalacia/tissue loss
- Marked asymmetry corresponding to old lesion side
- Widened cortical sulci / generalized atrophy
- No LP response x2
- No convincing DESH
- Ventricular shape distorted by prior hemorrhage/surgery

## Risk factors making shunt less attractive
- Ex-vacuo pattern
- Fragile post-hemorrhagic brain/vessels
- Prior surgery
- MCS with uncertain measurable benefit
- Overdrainage risk
- Subdural hematoma/hygroma risk
- Infection risk
- Need for revision/valve adjustments

## Decision-support output categories
- Strongly favors shunt-responsive hydrocephalus
- Mixed / uncertain
- Favors ex-vacuo; shunt benefit expected low
- Unsafe to conclude; needs clinician review

## Dad-specific default weighting
LP negative x2 and asymmetric ventricular enlargement near chronic basal ganglia/periventricular injury should weigh strongly against shunt-responsive NPH unless new imaging shows active hydrocephalus red flags.
