# Doctor Consultation Checklist: NPH vs Ex Vacuo

## Core diagnostic questions
1. Are the high-convexity sulci tight/effaced or widened?
2. Is there a DESH pattern?
3. Are the Sylvian fissures disproportionately enlarged?
4. Is the ventricular enlargement symmetric or asymmetric?
5. Does the enlarged ventricle expand into the region of encephalomalacia/atrophy?
6. Is the CT/MRI report describing brain atrophy with widened sulci?
7. Is there any evidence of obstructive hydrocephalus or CSF flow blockage?
8. Is there transependymal CSF edema, or is the periventricular change chronic gliosis?
9. What is the callosal angle on coronal reconstruction?
10. What is the Evans index and has it progressed over time?

## CSF response questions
1. What was the LP opening pressure?
2. How much CSF was removed?
3. Was there objective improvement after LP?
4. If no improvement after repeated LP, why is shunt still expected to help?
5. Can CSF infusion test/Rout be performed?
6. Can external lumbar drainage be used before permanent shunt?

## Surgery-risk questions
1. What is the estimated probability of meaningful improvement in wakefulness/function?
2. What is the estimated risk of overdrainage?
3. What is the estimated risk of subdural hematoma in this patient with atrophy/ex vacuo features?
4. What valve model will be used?
5. Will it be programmable?
6. Will it include anti-siphon or gravitational protection?
7. What initial pressure setting will be used and why?
8. What is the plan if there is no improvement?
9. Can the valve be set to maximum resistance if overdrainage occurs?
10. What is the follow-up CT/MRI schedule?

## Decision accountability question
If the surgeon recommends shunt, ask:

> Given the negative LP results, widened sulci/atrophy, and suspected ex vacuo pattern, what objective evidence suggests that this patient has shunt-responsive hydrocephalus rather than passive ventricular enlargement from tissue loss?
